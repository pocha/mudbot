Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026

What we collect

We collect only what is strictly necessary:

  • Your email address — used solely to send you a one-time login link. It is never stored on our servers after the email is dispatched.
  • Your PIN code / postal code — used to determine your country for residential proxy routing. Stored in encrypted form and never shared.

How your data is stored

All data associated with your account is encrypted using AES-256 with your personal auth token as the key. That token is generated when you register and delivered to you via email — it is never stored on our servers in recoverable form. Only a one-way SHA-256 hash of your token is retained for authentication.

This means: even the server operator cannot read your data. Your WhatsApp session credentials, your message schedules, and your recipient lists are all encrypted at rest and accessible only to someone who holds your token.

WhatsApp session

When you scan the QR code to connect your WhatsApp account, a session credential file is created locally. This file is immediately encrypted with a key derived from your token and the plaintext version is deleted. We never transmit your WhatsApp credentials anywhere other than to WhatsApp's own servers during normal operation.

Watobot sends messages on your behalf using your linked account. It does not read, store, or process any incoming messages.

Residential proxy routing

To protect your WhatsApp account from being flagged, each connection is routed through a dedicated residential IP address near your geographic location. Your IP address is not logged or stored beyond what is needed to assign an appropriate proxy region.

Cookies & tracking

We do not use cookies, tracking pixels, or any third-party analytics. Your auth token is stored in your browser's localStorage — it never leaves your device except when making API calls to this server.

Data retention

Your encrypted data persists on the server until you explicitly log out and confirm device removal. Logging out deletes your encrypted WhatsApp session. Re-registering with the same email generates a new token, which immediately invalidates the old one and makes all previously encrypted files permanently inaccessible.

Open source

Watobot is open source. The complete server-side code — including all encryption logic — is publicly auditable at github.com/pocha/mudbot. You are encouraged to review it.

Contact

Questions about privacy? Reach out via WhatsApp during support hours (9 AM – 7 PM IST, Mon–Sat) or open an issue on GitHub.
